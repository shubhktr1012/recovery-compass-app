2 — Lesson

Context
Why timing matters

Urges often show up at familiar times. Morning. Evening. After work. Before sleep.

These patterns don't mean you want to smoke or drink. They usually mean your body expects a routine.

Today, you're learning to recognize those moments — not fight them.

Once you notice the pattern, its power begins to dissolve.

3 — Action step

1

Morning Sunlight Exposure

10–20 minutes
  • Within 30 minutes of waking, go outside
  • Do not wear sunglasses
  • Face the direction of the sky
  • Stay outside for 10–20 minutes
Why this works

Morning sunlight resets the brain's circadian clock, helping the body release melatonin naturally at night.

✦

If sunlight is weak, increase exposure to 20–30 minutes.
